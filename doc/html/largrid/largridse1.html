1 Introduction

This report aims to discuss the design and the implementation of the largrid module of the LAR-CC library, including also the Cartesian product of general cellular complexes. In particular, we show that both n-dimensional grids of (hyper)-cuboidal cells and their d-dimensional skeletons (0 ≤ d ≤ n), embedded in En, may be properly and efficiently generated by assembling the cells produced by a number n of either 0- or 1-dimensional cell complexes, that in such lowest dimensions coincide with simplicial complexes.

In Section 2 we give the simple implementation of generation of lower-dimensional (say, either 0- or 1-dimensional) regular cellular complexes with integer coordinates. In Section 3 a functional decomposition of the generation of either full-dimensional cuboidal complexes in En and of their d-skeletons (0 ≤ d ≤ n) is given, showing in particular that every skeleton can be efficiently generated as a partition in cell subsets produced by the Cartesian product of a proper disposition of 0-1 complexes, according to the binary representation of a subset of the integer set {0,2n}. In Section 4 we provide a very simple and general implementation of the topological product of two cellular complexes of any topology. When applied to embedded linear cellular complexes (i.e. when the coordinates of 0-cells of arguments are fixed and given) the algorithm produces a Cartesian product of its two arguments. In Section 5 the exporting of the module to different languages is provided. The Section 6 contains the unit tests associated to the various algorithms, that are exported by the used literate environment in the proper test subdirectory—depending on the implementation language. In Section 7 the indexing structure of the macro sources and variables is exposed by the sake of the reader. In the Appendix, the Section A contains some programming utilities possibly needed by the developers.
